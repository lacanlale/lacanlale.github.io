About

Hi! My name is Jonathan Lacanlale and I am currently in the process of finishing my undergraduate degree at Cal State University, Northridge. Over the course of my computer science degree (and minor in mathematics) I was fortunate to receive experience been in and outside of the classroom.

For coursework, I've taken a number of classes relevant to my work and research interests including (but not limited to) software engineering, linear algebra, machine learning, databases, and several more. This is further supported by my research experience which I began during my third year of undergrad (2018-2019.) This started out under the mentorship of Dr. Ani Nahapetian, a mobile computing and HCI researcher, and was made possible through NIH-funded BUILD PODER. Through this, I've been fortunate to explore my research interests while also attending conferences to network with new peers within my field. Throughout my three years of experience I've been fortunate to work with amazing professors like Dr. Arun Kumar at UC San Diego (through STARS) and Dr. Stephen Huang at the University of Houston (through the NSF-Summer REU Site).

I have currently been working with Dr. Katya Mkrtchyan at Cal State University, Northridge, where we work on applied object detection and object tracking.
