Classification of Long Stepping Stone Connections

Duration: Summer 2020 (June - Early August)

Completed (?): Ongoing, however I am not active in this lab.

Research Mentor: Dr. Stephen Huang

Summary: I was fortunate to work on this project at the University of Houston's NSF-REU Site. As shown from the picture above, it was held virtually due to COVID-19. This project focused network security, a field that I have not previously studied before. Admittedly, this field is not in my primary interests, however working within the lab granted me an opportunity to experience a new workflow and transferable work experience.

The goal of this project is stepping stone connection identification. Stepping stone connection chains are commonly used as a means of concealing identity. For this, a hacker may use a long connection chain when attempting to connect to a target server. In our work, we provide evidence that these long connection chains have a significant difference from immediate, short connections to a server.
